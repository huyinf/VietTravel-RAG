The sand dunes have existed for thousands of years, shaped by natural forces and local climate. Over time, the area has attracted travelers due to its stunning beauty and recreational opportunities. Today, it remains a key part of Vietnam's tourism landscape, drawing visitors looking to explore its natural wonders.

[H2] 4. Poshanu Cham Towers

[P] The Poshanu Cham Towers, located in Bình Thuận, Vietnam, are a remarkable testament to the ancient Cham civilization that once thrived in this region. Perched on a hill overlooking the beautiful coastline, these towers date back to the 8th century and are dedicated to the worship of Shiva, the Hindu god. Visitors to the site are greeted by three main towers, each showcasing intricate carvings and architectural styles that reflect the rich cultural heritage of the Cham people.
[P] One of the most striking features of the Poshanu Cham Towers is their unique brick construction, which has withstood the test of time. The towers are not only significant for their historical value but also for their scenic beauty, offering panoramic views of the surrounding area. The site is a popular destination for both history enthusiasts and those seeking a peaceful retreat amidst stunning landscapes.
[LI] Ancient architecture dating back to the 8th century.
[LI] Beautiful carvings and sculptures of Hindu deities.
[LI] Scenic views of the coastline and surrounding hills.
[P] The Poshanu Cham Towers are famous for their stunning architecture and historical significance. They are a key representation of Cham culture, attracting tourists and researchers alike who are interested in ancient civilizations. The towers are also known for their cultural festivals, where traditional Cham music and dance performances often take place, enriching the visitor experience.
[P] The history of the Poshanu Cham Towers is deeply intertwined with the Cham Kingdom, which flourished in Southeast Asia between the 2nd and 15th centuries. These towers were built during the reign of the Champa Kingdom and served as religious monuments. Over the centuries, the site has faced challenges, including natural erosion and the impacts of war, but it has been preserved as a significant cultural heritage site. Today, it stands as a symbol of resilience and a reminder of the rich history of the Cham people.
[P] The best time to visit the Poshanu Cham Towers is during the dry season, from November to April. During this period, the weather is pleasant, with less humidity and lower chances of rain, making it ideal for exploring the site and taking in the breathtaking views. Early morning or late afternoon visits are particularly recommended to avoid the midday heat and to experience the magical atmosphere as the sun rises or sets over the towers.

[H2] 7. Ke Ga Lighthouse
[H3] Overview
[H3] Famous For
[H3] History
[H3] Best Time to Visit
[P] Ke Ga Lighthouse, also known as Kê Gà Lighthouse, is a stunning maritime landmark located in Bình Thuận Province, Vietnam. Perched on a rocky outcrop off the coast, this lighthouse stands as a sentinel for sailors navigating the South China Sea. Built in 1899 by the French, it is one of the oldest lighthouses in Vietnam and continues to play a crucial role in ensuring safe passage for vessels in the region.
[P] The lighthouse itself is an architectural marvel, rising to a height of 35 meters with a distinctive conical shape. Its white and red stripes make it a picturesque sight against the backdrop of the azure sea and rugged coastline. Visitors can climb the 183 steps to the top for a panoramic view of the surrounding landscape, making it a popular destination for both tourists and photography enthusiasts.
[P] Surrounded by clear waters, sandy beaches, and stunning rock formations, Ke Ga Lighthouse is not only a beacon of light but also a hub for adventure. Activities such as snorkeling, swimming, and exploring the nearby islands add to the allure of this remarkable location.
[P] Ke Ga Lighthouse is famous for:
[LI] Its historical significance as one of Vietnam's oldest lighthouses.
[LI] The breathtaking views from its observation deck.
[LI] Its scenic surroundings, including beautiful beaches and unique rock formations.
[LI] Being a popular spot for photography and maritime activities.
[P] The history of Ke Ga Lighthouse dates back to the late 19th century when it was constructed by French colonialists to aid navigation in the treacherous waters of the South China Sea. The lighthouse was built using bricks imported from France, and its design reflects the architectural style of that era. Over the years, Ke Ga Lighthouse has withstood the test of time and natural elements, becoming a symbol of maritime safety in Vietnam.
[P] In the present day, the lighthouse has been restored and is maintained by the Vietnamese government, continuing to serve its purpose while attracting visitors interested in history and culture.
[P] The best time to visit Ke Ga Lighthouse is during the dry season, which typically runs from November to April. During these months, the weather is pleasant, with less humidity and minimal rainfall, providing ideal conditions for sightseeing and outdoor activities. The clear skies and calm seas also enhance the stunning views from the lighthouse.
[P] Visiting during this period allows travelers to fully appreciate the beauty of the surrounding landscape and enjoy the various water sports available in the area.
